KOALA

Koalas are marsupials native to Australia, known for their distinctive appearance and tree-dwelling habits. They have a stout, tailless body, large head, and round, fluffy ears. Koalas primarily feed on eucalyptus leaves, which are toxic to most animals but provide the koala with the necessary nutrients.

These animals are mostly nocturnal and spend most of their time sleeping in trees, conserving energy. Koalas have a specialized digestive system that allows them to break down the tough eucalyptus leaves and extract the maximum amount of nutrients.

Koalas are social animals and communicate through a variety of vocalizations, including grunts and bellows. They are currently listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss, bushfires, and disease.
