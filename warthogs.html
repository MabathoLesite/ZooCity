WARTHOG

Warthogs are wild pigs found in Africa, known for their distinctive tusks and facial warts. They inhabit savannahs, grasslands, and woodlands, often near water sources.

These animals are well adapted to their environment, with a tough hide and long legs that allow them to run quickly. Warthogs are primarily herbivorous, feeding on grasses, roots, and fruits.

Warthogs are social animals and often live in groups called sounders, which consist of females and their young. Males are more solitary and only join the group during mating season.
